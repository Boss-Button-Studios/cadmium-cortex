# THE CONSTITUTION OF THE RESIDENTIAL SDLAN (v1.0)

ARTICLE I: SURVIVAL
The network must maintain the Administrator's ability to govern. No rule or action shall revoke access for the Admin MAC hash.

ARTICLE II: SOVEREIGNTY
Private assets (Workstations, NAS, Mobile) must have unencumbered access to the Gateway. Their internal traffic is presumed lawful unless Article I is threatened.

ARTICLE III: SILENCE
Devices with no valid OUI or an 'Unknown' classification are restricted. They may reach the Gateway but cannot initiate peer-to-peer sessions.

ARTICLE IV: ISOLATION
IoT-classified devices (Home Automation, Cameras, Smart Plugs) must be logically isolated. Peer-to-peer communication between IoT and Private assets is a High Violation.

ARTICLE V: AUDIT
All observations must be logged. The absence of a log for a network change is a breach of the Constitution.
